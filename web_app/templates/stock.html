What is a stock market? Click "Learn More"

A stock market is a public marketplace for the trading of company stock and derivatives at an agreed price. It’s a place where buyers and sellers meet to trade in securities such as stocks. The price of stocks changes based on supply and demand, the company’s performance, economic conditions, and other factors that might not seem rational—like investor “sentiment”—but all of which must be considered if you’re buying or selling shares.

Recognizing Wallstreet Center of the riches.

Physically, Wall Street refers to a street in New York City in lower Manhattan. It is also referred to as “the Financial District” because it houses multiple financial institutions and big banks. Additionally, the US Central Bank, the Federal Reserve, and the New York Stock Exchange (one of the largest exchanges globally) are located there. Figuratively, however, Wall Street is used to symbolize the financial markets, investments, and publicly-traded companies, even if they aren't physically situated on the street itself. Some even argue that Wall Street has become synonymous with the US financial system.
